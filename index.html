A Letter from the Secretary-General

Dear Delegates, faculty advisors, and future diplomats

The twelfth Session of the Hwa Chong Conflict Resolution and Inquiry (HCCRI) will be held from 29 May to 30 May in an entirely physical format at Hwa Chong Institution.

Established in 2011, HCCRI is one of Singapore's leading and most prestigious Model United Nations (MUN) conferences in the pre-university circuit. The two-day event allows delegates to delve into the intricacies of a wide range of global and national issues, working together with other like-minded peers to navigate the sophisticated policy-making process of the political realms.

At HCCRI, we aim to provide all delegates with a rewarding and enriching experience, regardless of experience level. For newer delegates, our beginner committees and delegate training allow you to debate on intricate global issues with much support and guidance. For the more experienced delegates, our intermediate and advanced councils, such as the Rajya Sabha, will provide you with much academic rigour while navigating the various special rules of procedures. Besides the academic components, our unique special projects initiatives, such as inter-council activities and Socials Night, will provide all delegates with an enjoyable experience throughout the two days.

On behalf of the Secretariat, we are delighted to announce that registration is now open for HCCRI XII. This document contains further information regarding our conference and how our conference will enrich your MUN experience. Should we require further clarification, do email us hccri.secretariat@gmail.com.

We look forward to seeing you at the 12th edition of HCCRI!

Best Regards,
Jonathan Lee
Secretary-General

HCCRI 2023: In motion, Chaos

Climate change, rising economic uncertainties, heightened international tensions - we are confronted with a world riddled with various challenges that have the ability to disrupt our lives in unfathomable fashions. As mankind advances, we are bound to face numerous waves of such “chaos”. Rather than shying away from them, the world - masses, politicians, nations - have to work together to confront them, as one. HCCRI 2023 aims to expose delegates to a plethora of geopolitical issues that plague our world and to gain insights into the conundrum faced on the national and international stage in the world of policymaking. HCCRI 2023 hopes to empower delegates to embody the spirit of international collaboration, working together to derive their own innovative solutions rather than riding the tide. The chaos we face may be inevitable, but it is not unsolvable. Will you take up the challenge, to ride the wave of chaos, and set a new world in motion.
